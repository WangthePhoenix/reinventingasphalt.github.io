Overview

Discarded rubber tyres are flooding the landfills.

In order to help the situation, there are asphalt mixes that use rubber tyres as ingredients.

However, these mixes are expensive and have extremely limited shelf-life.

A new mix is being developed to solve those problems.

The Ingredients

Crumb Rubber (CRMA)

Discarded rubber tyres are recycled from landfills to be ground down into a fine powder. This powder, when added to an asphalt mix (CRMA) will increase its stiffness. However, this mix suffers 2 major disadvantages.

  • CRMA requires a high temperature for production. This is expensive and creates noxious fumes.
